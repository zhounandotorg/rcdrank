Overview

 Regulated cell death is an essential biological process for physiological growth and development.

 RCD modalities are defined by distinctive morphological changes but they can crosstalk through interconnecting, even overlapping, signaling pathways.

 Multiple RCD forms may be induced simultaneously or successively, affecting different cell types or the same cell type synchronously or sequentially.

 Different forms of regulated cell death may coexist simultaneously in the same or different cell types, or be activated sequentially even in the same individual.

 Interventions that target secondary or minor forms, rather than the predominant form of cell death of the disease might still provide benefit.

 Determining which RCD modalities are most relevant or active within specific biological contexts remains a challenge.
